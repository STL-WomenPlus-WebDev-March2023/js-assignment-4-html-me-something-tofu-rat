Ocean Animals!

A Few of My Favorite Ocean Animals

A Note From the Author:

Hello, and thank you for visiting my site! My name is Moe, and I have had a fascination with ocean life for as long as I can remember. Here is a curated list of some of my favorite creatures you can find in the ocean <3

Mola Mola

The Mola Mola is an odd fish, to say the least. Often found floating lazily at the surface in open ocean water, these are some of the world's largest bony fish. While we commonly refer to this fish as an ocean sunfish in English, other languages have more creative names. In Polish, they are called "samogłów", which means "only head", or "head alone". My personal favorite is the Russian name, "рыба-луна" (pronounced roughly as ryba-luna), which translates to "moon fish".

Orcas

Orcas have been popping up a lot in the news lately. Despite a lot of bad press, these guys remain one of my all time favorite animals. The term "killer whale" comes from a mistranlastion of the orignal Spanish name which translates to "whale killer"- a more accurate description, as orcas are known to hunt whale species. Many know that orcas are the largest members of the dolphin family, but they are also whales. While all dolphins are whales, not all whales are dolphins. All species are related and fall under the order "Cetacea". All things considered, these animals are incredibly intelligent and downright frightening at times. Their black and white faces remind me of corpse paint- to me, they are the most metal animals in the sea.

Cyerce Elegans

Little is known about the lives of this species, so there is little to report on here. What is obvious, is that these ethereal creatures look like they came straight out of a sci-fi movie. Truly, few creatures can compete with Cyerce Elegans beauty.

Belugas

Did you know? These whales are actually born grey. It can take up to eight years for them to turn white. These whales also have the unique talent of swimming backwards. And finally, belugas living in captivity have been reported to mimic the speech of their human handlers. Noc, a beluga whale who lived at the National Marine Mammal Foundation in San Diego for 30 years, was one of the first belugas observed to exhibit this type of behavior. Noc managed to convince a human diver to climb out of the whale’s tank when he heard what he thought was another person instructing him to leave. It turned out that the sound had actually been Noc mimicking the word “out”. source: us.whales.org

Manatees

Look at this guy!!! Are you kidding me?! Anyway, many people know that these cuties are thought to be some of the earliest inspiration for (or mistakenly identified as) mermaids. But did you know that these gentle creatures are more closely related to elephants than other marine mammals? They also cannot turn their heads to look at things. Lacking the proper vertebrae to turn their heads, manatees must turn their whole body if they wish to look at something out of view.

That's all folks! Thank you for taking some time to get to know some of my favorite marine animals.
